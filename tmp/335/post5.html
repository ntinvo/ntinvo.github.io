Video Games' privacy and security. Things we need to know when playing video games.

Posted by Tin Vo on November 29, 2016

This is a pretty interesting project for me because I can actually show what I had made with everyone in class. I got these videos when I visited my aunt in May 2016. Then I decided to cut/merged all of these long videos into a shorter one and added sound track to it to be more fun. The sound track that I used was Waking Dreams - Transcend, which is free to use. Anyone can use this video as long as they credit me as the owner of this video (if interested). By the way, this was at Dam Site Marina in Heber Springs, AR.

Video of me having fun with family at Dam Site Marina in May 2016.

Additionally, to me, sharing my work is okay as long as people who use my work cite that the source code is from me. As a computer science major, I like the idea of sharing my work (the program source code like open source that we have learned) as well as using other people’s source code because it helps me improve my code/program such as bugs detection, program optimization, etc thank to the helps of all the programmers/developers. However, if my work is used by someone, and I do not receive credit for it; then, I would be really sad because it takes lots of time and effort to create it. So for this, I think I would go with Attribution ShareAlike license; it states that “This license lets others remix, tweak, and build upon your work even for commercial purposes, and long as they credit you and license their new creations under the identical terms” (Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International). The reason that I chose this license is because I feel like if people has some better ideas (This is most likely the case because more people will have more and better ideas) based on my work, they can use it as a foundation to create a better work; however, they should state that they use my work.
